METABOLITE CHANNELING WITH DNA ORIGAMI - OVERVIEW

The immobilization of enzymes on DNA origami is known to increase metabolic activity through increasing proximity of enzymes in multi-enzyme pathways, thus reducing diffusion distances for metabolites 1. Enzyme ratio has been shown to have some effect on metabolic activity 2. Although the greatest rate enhancement is observed due to immobilisation to the DNA origami alone, a proportionally smaller increase can be observed from the proximity of enzymes to each other within a system 3 up to 5nm 4. The effect of enzyme orientation on metabolic rate has not been extensively investigated to the best of our knowledge.



NANOTUBE STRUCTURE DESIGN

Initially, we planned to immobilize our enzymes inside a nanotubule formed of a single layer of helical bundles. However, after thermodynamic modelling we found this structure to lack stability due to the diameter required to incorporate multiple enzymes inside the tube. We discovered that we would need a modular nanotubule structure with more redundancy to withstand the kinetic activities taking place inside our metabolite channeling system.
We decided that a reliable initial model would be one highly similar to the modular DNA Origami nanotubule structure for Glucose Oxidase (GOx) and Horseradish Peroxidase (HP) published by Linko, Eerikäinen & Kostiainen in 2015 5. The published modular structure was ideal for a two stage catalytic system such as our own with modular reaction chambers.

Our initial assembly involved the use of the same staples as by Linko, Eerikäinen & A. Kostiainen, but a different the method for enzyme immobilisation due to lab reagents available. In place of a biotin enzyme immobilisation system, we modified enzyme base oligonucleotides with NTA-Maleimide to allow immobilisation of PETase and MHETase to these strands in the centre of the nanotubule by their 6His-Tag 6.
