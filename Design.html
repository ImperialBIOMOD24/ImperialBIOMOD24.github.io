NANOTUBE STRUCTURE DESIGN

"Initially, we planned to immobilize our enzymes inside a nanotubule formed of a single layer of helical bundles. However, after thermodynamic modelling we found this structure to lack stability due to the diameter required to incorporate multiple enzymes inside the tube. We discovered that we would need a modular nanotubule structure with more redundancy to with stand the kinetic activities taking place inside our metabolite channeling system. We decided that a reliable initial model would be one highly similar to the modular DNA Origami nanotuble structure for Glucose Oxidase (GOx) and Horseradish Peroxidase (HP) pulished by Linko, Eerikäinen & A. Kostiainen in 2015 . The published modular structure was ideal for a two stage catalytic system such as our own with modular reaction chambers.

Our initial assembly involved the use of the same staples as by Linko, Eerikäinen & A. Kostiainen, but a different the method for enzyme immobilisation due to lab reagents available. In place of a biotin enzyme immobilisation system, we modified enzyme base oligonucleotides with NTA-Maleimide to allow immoilisation of PETase and MHETase to these strands in the centre of the nanotubule by their 6His-Tag. (Rp et al., 2009) "
