The Novel IsPETase-MHETase Cascade.

BACKGROUND

Of the 450 million tonnes of plastic produced globally every year, less than 10% is estimated to be recycled1. Insufficient and expensive recycling technologies limit the proportion of plastics which can be repurposed into new materials, while lack of economic incentive has encouraged the uptake of less sustainable waste management strategies2. The IsPETase-MHETase cascade is a new low energy recycling technology which is able to degrade PET to useful products through the manipulation of cellular machinery. Enzymes IsPETase and IsMHETase are immobilised on DNA origami modular nanotubes to enhance degradation of PET via metabolite channeling to ethylene glycol and terephthalate These products can be separated into ethylene glycol, a useful chemical agent in variety of commercial applications including antifreeze and break-fluid products3, while both chemicals in solution can be used to synthesize new PET4. We hope that our technology may be used in the development of novel, low energy plastic recycling techniques.

PROBLEM
OBJECTIVES

To create a PET cell-free degradation system using DNA Origami and IsPETase and MHETase, which allows BHET to be degraded to ethylene glycol and terepthalic acid in solution.

GOALS

Assess the feasibility of cascade design to explore optimal geometry using mathematical modelling.

Produce IsPETase and IsMHETase in E.Coli with pET28+ vector.

Demonstrate the synthesis and assembly of DNA origami module units.

Assemble and confirm formation of cascade without addition of enzymes.

Demonstrate the modification of enzyme linker oligonucleotides with NTA.

Demonstrate the assembly of individual DNA origami module units with NTA-modified enzyme linker oligonucleotides.

Demonstrate the immobilization of PETase and MHETase on each respective DNA origami module (PET, MHET).

Demonstrate formation of cascade by assembly of PETase and MHETase origami modules.
