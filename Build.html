IsPETase AND IsMHETase SYNTHESIS

The gene expression sequences for Ideonella Sakaiensis PETase and MHETase were found from previous gene expression studies (1,2). PETase was expressed with a lamB signal peptide from maltoporin protein, promoting a Sec-dependent pathway and localisation to the outer e.coli membrane as this has been shown to increase secretion and yield of protein, 3. Similar studies could not be found for IsMHETase, thus the protein was expressed with its native signal peptide as described in Janatunaim & Fibrani’s 2020 study.

MATERIALS AND METHODS
  • Amino acid sequences were checked against PDB sequences (6ILW for PETase, 6QZ4 for MHETase), using Vector Builder Sequence Alignment Tool. As the PETase sequence had already been codon-optimised in the Joo. et al 2018 study, only MHETase required codon optimisation which was performed with the IDT Biosciences codon optimisation tool.

    •
    1. A pUCIDT +Kan plasmid with each gene of interest were ordered from IDT Biosciences on glycerol stock.
    2. Plasmids were spread on LB agar and incubated at 37ºC overnight.
    3. Two colonies were selected from each gene plate and incubated individually in 5ml LB broth with Kanamycin, with vigorous shaking at 37ºC overnight.
    4. A miniprep was then performed on each of the 4 samples before DNA extract was added to 4 identical liquid E. coli BL21 DE3 cultures and spread over LB agar with Kanamycin and cultured at 37ºC overnight.
    5. One plate for each gene was successful; one colony from each successful plate was then cultured in 100ml LB broth with Kanomycin, with vigorous shaking at 37ºC overnight.
    6. Each culture was then made up to 1L and grown to an optical density of 0.6 and 0.8, after approximately 90 minutes.
    7. Both proteins were than purified as described in 3, after which assay with SDS-PAGE was performed to confirm protein production.
