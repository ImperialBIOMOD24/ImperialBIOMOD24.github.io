IsPETase AND IsMHETase SYNTHESIS

The gene expression sequences for Ideonella Sakaiensis PETase and MHETase were found from previous gene expression studies (1,2). PETase was expressed with a lamB signal peptide from maltoporin protein, promoting a Sec-dependent pathway and localisation to the outer e.coli membrane as this has been shown to increase secretion and yield of protein, 3. Similar studies could not be found for IsMHETase, thus the protein was expressed with its native signal peptide as described in Janatunaim & Fibrani’s 2020 study.

MATERIALS AND METHODS
  • Amino acid sequences were checked against PDB sequences (6ILW for PETase, 6QZ4 for MHETase), using Vector Builder Sequence Alignment Tool. As the PETase sequence had already been codon-optimised in the Joo. et al 2018 study, only MHETase required codon optimisation which was performed with the IDT Biosciences codon optimisation tool.

    •
    1. A pUCIDT +Kan plasmid with each gene of interest were ordered from IDT Biosciences on glycerol stock.
    2. Plasmids were spread on LB agar and incubated at 37ºC overnight.
    3. Two colonies were selected from each gene plate and incubated individually in 5ml LB broth with Kanamycin, with vigorous shaking at 37ºC overnight.
    4. A miniprep was then performed on each of the 4 samples before DNA extract was added to 4 identical liquid E. coli BL21 DE3 cultures and spread over LB agar with Kanamycin and cultured at 37ºC overnight.
    5. One plate for each gene was successful; one colony from each successful plate was then cultured in 100ml LB broth with Kanomycin, with vigorous shaking at 37ºC overnight.
    6. Each culture was then made up to 1L and grown to an optical density of 0.6 and 0.8, after approximately 90 minutes.
    7. Both proteins were than purified as described in 3, after which assay with SDS-PAGE was performed to confirm protein production.


    DNA ORIGAMI

    SYNTHESIS

    The synthesis and assembly of the DNA origami PETase and MHETase module units comprising the cascade was adapted from Linko et al. 4.

    Staple sequence strands were purchased from IDT. For each of the PETase and MHETase module units, 3 staple strands contained a C6-amine modification (5AmMC6) on the 5’ end, intended to act as enzyme linkers for downstream enzyme immobilisation. All strands were first centrifuged (2 mins, 3000 xg) and subsequently resuspended in an amount of 1x TE buffer (10mM pH 8.0 Tris-HCl, 0.1mM EDTA; filter-sterilised 0.22µm filter) to achieve a final concentration of 100µM. Resuspension was achieved by a combination of centrifugation (2 mins, 3000 xg) and vortexing (1000 rpm) to ensure homogeneity. Oligonucleotides were then stored at -20◦C to maximise stability 5.

    Concentration was then verified by measuring the absorbance at 260nm (A260) using the NanoDrop™ One Spectrophotometer, assuming a standard ssDNA conversion factor of 33µg/A260, where \[ \text{Concentration}, c \, [\mu\text{g/mL}] = A_{260} \times \text{Conversion Factor} \] and using the Beer-Lambert Law, \[ A_{260} = \varepsilon_{260} \times c \times p \] where \[ \varepsilon_{260} \] is the extinction coefficient [L/mole.cm] and \[ \text{p} \] is the length of light path through sample [cm]. Given some discrepancies between the expected and measured oligo concentration, this was in part likely due to the conversation factor being oligo-specific, which is known to vary between sequence context and base composition 6.

    . ASSEMBLY

    To synthesise and verify assembly of each module unit, staple strands (including unmodified enzyme linker strands) corresponding to that of the PETase and MHETase structures were mixed and diluted to achieve a final concentration of 500nM.

    For PET Module Unit:

  • 5µL of each staple strand (total: 183 strands), topped up with 85µL Ultrapure water
  • 5µL of each staple strand (total: 187 strands), topped up with 65µL Ultrapure water


    • To test for the optimal Mg2+ ion concentration to achieve optimal folding, DNA origami was folded under 4 different conditions, with varying concentration of MgCl2.

    Each condition was made by mixing an amount of scaffold, staples, and folding buffer (1x TAE buffer (40mM pH 8.0 Tris-HCl, 19mM acetic acid, 1mM EDTA), MgCl2, NaCl) together to achieve 50µL of DNA solution.

    Each DNA origami solution was then annealed over 2 folding temperature ramps (75 minutes, 65-60◦C; 57 hrs, 59-40◦C).

    Agarose gel electrophoresis was then conducted to verify the quality of and purify the folded origami. Samples were run in 1% agarose gel, prepared by mixing 1g agarose with 100mL 0.1x TAE buffer, and then microwaved for 3 minutes. Once cooled, 30µL of Ethidium bromide solution (0.0625µg/L) was added. The gel was then cast into a gel box with combs. For use as a reference, M13mp18 scaffold strands (7249 bp) were diluted to 50nM, and 1kb DNA ladders were diluted 1 in 10, both with 0.1x TAE buffer. 5µL of each DNA solution, scaffold and DNA ladder were added to 1µL of 6X Purple Loading Dye. The gel was run for 40 minutes at 120V. Gel images were then captured by a Gel Doc (iBright™ CL750 Imaging System) [ref figure in test section].
